    [ArXiv:2209.07537]The binary system of the spinning-top Be star Achernar

    by P. Kervella et al.

    Achernar, the closest and brighest classical Be star, presents rotational flattening, gravity darkening, occasional emission lines due to a gaseous disk, and an extended polar wind. It is aslo a member of a close binary system with an early A-type dwarf companion. Be stars may from through a direct, single-star evolution path without mass transfer.

  • Determine the orbital parameters of the Achernar system and to estimate the phyiscal properties of the components.
  • Instruments: VLT/VLTI (VISIR,NACO,SPHERE,AMBER,PIONIER,GRAVITY, and MATISSE)
  • Over a period of 13 years (2006-2019).
  • Achernar appears to be a promising progenitor of Cepheid binary systems.

    • [ArXiv:2209.07544]Resonant Production of Light Sterile Neutrinos in Compact Binary Merger Remnants

    by Garðar Sigurðarson et al.

    The existence of eV-mass sterile neutrinos is not ruled out because of persistent experimental anomalies. Upcoming multi-messenger detections of neutron-star merger remnants could provide indirect constraints on the existence of these particles. Their findings imply that the production of sterile stars can have indirect implications on the disk cooling rate, its outflows, and related electromagnetic observables which remain to be asssessed.

  • 1 active + 1 sterile species - a function of the sterile neutrino mixing parameters, neutrino emission angle from the accretion torus, and temporal evolution of the remnant.
  • As the black hole torus evolves in time, the shallower baryon density is responsible for more adiabatic flavor conversion, leading to larger regions of the mass-mixing parameter space being affected by flavor mixing.

    • [ArXiv:2209.07613]Galaxy mergers can rapidly shut down star formation

    by Sara L. Ellison et al.

    Galaxy mergers trigger both star formation and accertion onto the central supermassive black hole. As a result of subsequent energetic feedback processes, it has long been proposed that star formation may be promptly extinguished in galaxy merger remnants. Based on a sample of \(\sim 500\) identified from the Ultraviolet Near Infrared Optical Northern Survey (UNIONS), they show that the frequency of post-merger galaxies that have rapidly shutdown their star formation following a previous starburst is 30-60 times higher than expected from a control sample of non-merging galaxies. No such excess is found in a sample of close galaxy pairs, demonstrating that mergers can indeed lead to a rapid halt to star formation, but that this process only manifests after coalescence.

    [ArXiv:2209.06854]Snowmass Theory Frontier: Astrophysics and Cosmology

    by Daniel Green et al.

    They summarize progress made in theoretical astrophysics and cosmology over the past decade and areas of interest for the coming decade.

    The impact of theoretical effort in cosmology and astrophysics be viewed

  • advancing our understanding of fundamentral physics by forcing us to ponder extreme scenarios -- quantum effects and gravity
  • developing new microscopic models that can potentially explain the outstanding problems facing our understanding of nature
  • inventing new approaches to test our best-motivated theories, in addition to develoing the theoretical tools needed to properly interpret the resulting data.

    • The evidence for existing Dark Mark (DM):

  • CMB
  • Large scale structure and halo data
  • galaxy rotation curvues
  • stellar kinematics
  • weak and strong gravitational lensing

    • The DM mass is constrainted on the lower end to be larger than \(\sim 10^{-20} \) eV and on the upper end to be smaller than \(\sim 10^4 M_\odot\). The lower bound comes from the De Broglie wavelength ( \( \lambda_{\mathrm{DM}} \sim \mathrm{dV}/m_{\mathrm{DM}} \) mm) of the DM being galactic scale for DM masses \(m_{\mathrm{DM}} \sim 10^{-22} \) eV and typical galactic velocities.

    On the upper end of the mass spectrum,

  • Sufficiently compact DM with masses above \(\sim 10^{3} - 10^4 M_\odot \) would be too granular on astrophysical scales
  • DM masses above \(\sim 10^3 M_\odot\) have been suggested to lead to the distruption of globular cluster
  • while DM masses above \(\sim 10^4 M_\odot \) would likely inject too much Poisson noise in the Ly\(\alpha\) forest.

    • The QCD axion

  • It esd originally introduced to solve the strong-CP problem related to the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) but which may also explain the observed DM, is intimately connected to QCD, including lattice QCD, and the precision frontier.

    • The ``top-down" constructions

  • based on string theory. Axions may be connected to deep concepted in quantum gravity, such as extra dimensions and the weak gravity conjecture, and these connections have been sharpened over the past decade.
  • The QCD axion and axion-like particles, if the exist, may possibly be the only way of experimentally probing string theory conpactifications, if string theory is indeed realized in our Universe.

    • Sterile neutrino DM

  • It is naturally intertwined with the neutrino frontier, as \(\sim\)keV-scale sterile neutrinos would be connected to the active neutrinos through e.g. the see-saw mechanism.
  • A warm DM candidate.
  • Their finite temperature may wash out structure on small astrophysical scales.

    • Baryogenesis:

  • The present-day primordial baryon asymmetry is quantified precisely through big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) and CMB analyses to \(\eta_B = (n_b - n_{\bar{b}})/n_\gamma = n_b / n_\gamma = (6.10 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-10} \).

    • Axions:

  • Axions are light, bosonic particles that are part of a broader class of possible new physics candidates that includes dark photons and scalars, which could also be DM candidates.
  • Scalars and dark photons, especially in the context of wave-like DM, have also seen enormous theoretical progress over the past decade and also have compelling ultraviolet and phenomenological motivations.
  • QCD coupled to massive quarks has a CP-violating angle \(\bar{\theta}\) that generates a neutron electric dipole moment (EDM): \(d_n \sim 10 ^{-16} \mathrm{e \cdot cm}\). However, direct searches for the neutron EDM have yet to yield a non-zero value, constraining \(|d_n| \lesssim 3 \times 10^{-26} \mathrm{e \cdot cm}\), which implies that \( |\bar{\theta}| \lesssim 10^{-10}\). CP and P are not restored as symmetries of the SM if \(\bar{\theta} = 0\), making symmetry-based solutions to the strong-CP problem difficult.
  • The production mechamism for QCD axion DM depends crucially on the ordering of inflation relative to PQ symmetry breaking.
  • Axion-like particles, which do not couple to QCD but have small bare masses or small masses from confining dark sectors, may also make up a sizeable fraction of the DM.
  • The fuzzy DM modifies structure on astrophysical scales.
  • Fuzzy DM may also reduce the dynamical friction that objects feel in DM halo.
  • Fuzzy DM -- explain the lack of low-mass subhalos of the Milkly Way and the ``too-big-to-fail" problem associated with the lack of high-luminosity satellite galaxies.

    • Primordial non-Gaussianity

  • In large scale structure are protected from non-linear evolution for their own distinct reasons.
  • Local non-Gaussianity, parameterized by \(f_{\mathrm{NL}}^{\mathrm{loc}}\), arises in multi-field infaltion $$\Phi (\vec{x}) = \varphi_g (\vec{x}) - f_{\mathrm{NL}}^{\mathrm{loc}} \varphi_g^2 (\vec{x}) \cdots,$$ where \(\Phi(\vec{x}) \) is the Newtonian potential and \(\varphi_g (\vec{x}) \) is a Gaussian random field.
  • Non-Gaussianity statisties give rise to scale dependent bias, namely the density contrast of galaxies \(\delga_g(\vec{x}) \approx f_{\mathrm{NL}}^{\mathrm{loc}} \).
