    [ArXiv:2211.02129]High Redshift ΛCDM Cosmology: To Bin or not to Bin?

    by Eoin O Colg ´ ain et al.

  • Using the mock data and parameters from the CMB.
  • They find that PDF peaks only agree with the input parameters in low redshift ( \( z \lesssim 1 \) ) bins for \(H(z)\) and \(D_A(z)\) constraints are combined.
  • When input parameters are not recovered, they observe that PDFs exhibit non-Guassian tails towards larger \(\Omega_m\) values and shifts to (less pronounced) peaks at smaller \(\Omega_m\) values.
  • This flattening of the PDF is expected as \(H(z)\) and \(D_A(z)\) observations only constrain combiantions of cosmological parameters at higher redshifts, so uniform PDFs are expected.

    [ArXiv:2211.02376]Growth of A Massive Black Hole Via Tidal Disruption Accretion

    by Seungjae Lee et al.

  • TDA can enhance the MBH's growth rate by up to more than an order of magnitude.
  • The MBH may grow expeditiously with both GA an TDA, and the massive MBH could consume its neighboring gas faster than being replenished by gas inflows.
  • Their study demonstrates the need to consider different channels of black hole accretion that my provide clues for the extience of supermassive black holes at high redshift.

    • [ArXiv:2211.02473]Calibrating gamma-ray bursts by using Gaussian process with type Ia supernovae

    by Liang Nan et al.

    By using Gaussian process to reconstruct the smoothed luminosity distance from the Pantheon type Ia supernovae (SNe Ia) sample, we utilize the reconstructed results to calibrate the Ep-Eiso correlation with the Markov Chain Monte Carlo method and construct a Hubble diagram with the A220 GRB data, in which A118 GRB data with the higher qualities appropriate for cosmological purposes.

    [ArXiv:2211.02651]Did JWST observe imprints of axion miniclusters or primordial black holes?

    by Gert Hütsi et al.

  • The James Webb Space Telescope has detected surprisingly luminous early galaxies that indicate a tension with the ΛCDM.
  • The tension could be resolved by axion miniclusters or primordial black holes.

    • [ArXiv:2211.02539]TDI noises transfer functions for LISA

    by Dam Quang Nam et al.

  • The data streams to be used in order to extract the large number and variety of GW sources are Time-Delay Interferometry (TDI) data.
  • Using these transfer functions, realistic noise curves for various instrumental configurations are provided to data analysis algorithms and used for instrument design.

    • [ArXiv:2211.02586]Questions on calculation of primordial power spectrum with large spikes: the resonance model case

    by Keisuke Inomata et al.

  • Loop corrections are negligible when such a large amplifiction occurs during inflation.
  • Typical parameter space to the \(\mathcal{O}(10^7)\) amplifiction of the power spectrum required for a sufficient PBH production, the one-loop power spectrum dominates over the tree-level one, indicating the breakdown of the perturbation theory.
