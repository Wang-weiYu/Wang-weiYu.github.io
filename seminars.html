    • arXiv I interested everyday

    [ArXiv:2210.09530]Constraining Cosmological Parameters using the Cluster Mass-Richness Relation

    by Mohamed H. Abdullah et al.

    The cluster mass-richness relation (MRR) is an observationally efficient and potentially powerful cosmological tool for constraining the mean matter density of the universe and the amplitude of fluctuations using the cluster abundance technique.

  • GalWCat19, a publicly avialable galaxy cluster catalog they created from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey-DR13 spectroscopic dataset.
  • Their constraints on \(\Omega_m\) and \(\sigma_8\) are consistent and very competitive with the Planck2018 results.

    • [ArXiv:2210.09661]A crack in the track of the Hubble Constant

    by Marie Gueguen

    They sketch a methodological guide for identifying, quantifying and reducing uncertainties in astrophysical measurements.

    [ArXiv:2210.09697]Deep Investigation of Neutral Gas Origins (DINGO): HI stacking experiments with early science data

    by Jonghwan Rhee

  • The results show that galaxy morphology based optical colour is strongly linked to HI gas properties.
  • They find that the derived HI scaling relations are comparable to other published results, with consistent trends also observed to \(\sim 0.5\) dex lower limits in stellar mass, stellar surface density.
  • The cosmic HI densities derived from our data are consistent with other published value at similar redshift.

    • [ArXiv:2210.09737]Moving gravitational wave sources at cosmological distances: Impact on the measurement of the Hubble constant

    by Alejandro Torres-Orjuela and Xian Chen

    They found a motion of the source changes its apparent distance by a factor \(1 + z_\mathrm{rel}^2\) in contrast to a linear factor for the cosmological redshift. They consider the error introduced in the measurement of the Hubble constant using standard sirens for two cases:

  • When the effect of velocity on the redshift of the EM counterpart is considered but not on the apparent distance obtained from GWs.
  • When the effect of the velocity is ignored completely.
    • They find that in the first case the error can reach 1 \% for a source moving due to the peculiar velocity of its host galaxy and that in the second the error can be more than 5 \% for a source at the distance of GW150914 with the same velocity.

    [ArXiv:2210.09865]Revealing the effects of curvature on the cosmological models

    by Weiqiang Yang et al.

    Their main result is that a non-flat Universe cannot be discarded in light of the current astronomical data. Forcing the Universe to be flat can significantly bias the constraints on the equation of state of the DE component and its dynamical nature.

    [ArXiv:2210.03749]New Constraints on Dark Matter and Cosmic Neutrino Profiles through Gravity

    by Yu-Dai Tsai et al.

  • Gravitational constraints on dark matter and cosmic neutrino profiles in the solar system using asteroid (101955) Bennu.
  • A DM-SM long-range fifth force with strength \(\tilde{\alpha}_D\) times stronger than gravity. -- \(\rho_\mathrm{DM} \lesssim \bar{\rho}_\mathrm{DM} (6 \times 10^6 / \tilde{\alpha}_D) \).

    • [ArXiv:2210.09386]Breaking Bad Degeneracies with Love: Improving gravitational-wave measurements through universal relations

    by Yiqi Xie et al.

    A new parameterization of the tidal effects in the binary neutron star waveform, exploiting the binary Love relations, that breaks the distance-inclination degeneracy.

    [ArXiv:2210.09448]Dark Matter from Monogem

    by Christopher V. Cappiello et al.

    They show that the Monogem Ring supernova remnant is both close enough and the correct age to produce such a flux, and thus they produce novel direct detection constraints and sensitivities for future experiments.

    [ArXiv:2210.09732]Constraints on Late Time Violations of the Equivalence Principle in the Dark Sector

    by Cameron C. Thomas and Carsten van de Bruck

    The evolution of dark energy itself, leading to a violation of the equivalence principle in the dark sector at late times. They study a specific model and show that such late-time violations of the EP in the DM sector are not strongly constrained by the evolution of the cosmological background and by observables in the linear regime (e.g. from the cosmic microwave background radiation).

    • [ArXiv:2210.09487]The Effect of Gravitational Lensing on Fast Transient Event Rates

    by Mawson W. Sammons et al.

    For static lensing geometries, they rule out the prospect that all FRBs are gravitationally lensed for a range of lens masses and show that lens masses greater than \(10^{-5} M_\odot\) can be constrained with 8000 un-localised high fluence FRBs at 1.4GHz, as might be detected by the next generation of FRB-finding telescopes.

    by Veronica Dike et al.

  • Independet constraints.
  • They present an approach to constrain the PHB mass and mass fraction from the flux ratios of quadruply imaged quasars.
  • \(f_\mathrm{PBH} < 0.17 \) (95 \% C.L.)
