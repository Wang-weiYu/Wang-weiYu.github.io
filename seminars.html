    [ArXiv:2210.14258]Search for gravitational-wave transients associated with magnetar bursts during the third Advanced LIGO and Advanced Virgo observing run

    by Kara Merfeld (on behalf of the LIGO, Virgo, and KAGRA collaborations)

  • Megneters are neutron stars with exceptionally strong dipole magnetic fields which are observed to display a range of x-ray flaring behaviour, but the flaring mechanism is not well understood.
  • O3: magnetar SGR 1935+2154 and Swift J1818-1607.
  • Gravitational waves coincident with these magnetar flares with minimally modeled, coherent searches with specifically target both short-duration gravitational waves motivated by in the magnetar's core, as well as long-duration gravitational waves motivated by the Quasi-Periodic Oscillations observed in the tails of gaint flares.
  • Methods and sensitivity estiamtes of these searches.
    • Search Methods: 2, short duration (ms to s in duration) and long duration (100s of s) gravitational waves.
  • The short-duration: most sensitive to GWs that are produced by excited f-modes in the neutron star's core. 2 different short-duration: centered search and the delayed search.
  • The long-duration: conducted after the flare time and sensitive to GWs in the same frequency range as the QPOs. Stochastic Transient Analysis Multi-detector Pipeline (STAMP) Thrane et al.(2011)

    • [ArXiv:2210.14339]Converting dark matter to dark radiation does not solve cosmological tensions

    by Fiona McCarthy and J. Colin Hill

  • The potential for such a resolution, in which some fraction of the dark matter has converted into dark radiation since release of the CMB.
  • They find that such a model is effectively ruled out by low-\(\ell \) CMB data, in particular by the excess ISW singal caused by the additional dark energy density required to preserve flatness after dark matter conversion into dark radiation.
