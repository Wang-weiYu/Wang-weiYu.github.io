    • arXiv I interested everyday

    [ArXiv:2210.05718]Wave-optics limit of the stochastic gravitational wave background

    by Alice Garoffolo

  • A theoretical description of propagation effects.
  • The classical matter approximation to capture wave-optics effects such as interference and diffraction.
  • They build the two point correlation function describing the statistics of the tensor models of the SGWB, and introduce the gravitational and isotropic SGWB.

    • [ArXiv:2210.05777]Gas accretion and Ram Pressure Stripping of Haloes in Void Walls

    by B. B. Thompson et al.

  • They conduct hydrodynamical cosmological zoom simulations of fourteen voids to study the ability of halos to accrete gas at different locations throught the voids at \(z = 0\).
  • Fast flow haloes: A tenth of the haloes are expected to be unable to accrete external gas due to its fast flow passed them.
  • The numerical examples of haloes with a clear jelly-fish like gas morphology.

    • [ArXiv:2210.06125]Return of Harrison-Zeldovich spectrum in light of recent cosmological tensions

    by Jun-Qian Jiang et al.

  • They find strong evidence for a \(n_s = 1\) Universe.
  • base1000+ACT+SPT dataset.
  • No Hubble tension but hardly balanced by the shift of other cosmological parameters and exsiting \(\sigma_8\) tension.

    • [ArXiv:2210.06398]Prospects of testing late-time cosmology with weak lensing of gravitational waves and galaxy surveys

    by Anna Balaudo et al.

  • GW-WL offers the poten tial to become a cosmological probe complementary to LSS surveys, particularly for those parameters that cannot be constrained by other GW probes such as standard sirens.
  • \(\Lambda\)CDM and scalar-tensor cosmologies, using the Effective Field Theory formalism as a unifying language.

    • [ArXiv:2210.06412]Wash-in leptogenesis after axion inflation

    by Valerie Domcke et al.

    Their model is representative of a broader class of new leptogenesis scenarios and suggests interesting observational signatures with regard to intergalactic magnetic fields, primordial black holes, and gravitational waves.

    [ArXiv:2210.06178]Multi-messenger constraints on Abelian-Higgs cosmic string networks

    by Mark Hindmarsh and Jun'ya Kume

    we adopt a simple parametrisation of the AH string network allowing for both particle and gravitational wave production, which sets the basis for a "multi-messenger" investigation of this model.

  • For a DM mass of 500 GeV, the observed relic abundance can be explained by decaying AH strings that also account for the NANOGrav signal.
