    [ArXiv:2208.14088]The challenge of ruling out inflation via the primordial graviton background

    by Sunny Vagnozzi and Abraham Loeb

    They argue that a detection of the Cosmic Graviton Background (CGB), the relic radiation from gravitons decoupling around the Planck time, would rule out the inflationary paradigm, as realistic inflationary models would dilute the CGB to an unobservable level. The CGB contribution to the effective number of relativistic species, \(\Delta N_{\mathrm{eff},g} \approx 0.054 \), is within the reach of next-generation cosmological probes. They argue that detecting the high-frequency stochastic gravitational wave background wave background associated to the CGB will be challenging but potentially feasible.

    An inflationary phase taking place between the Planck era and today would wash out the CGB, rendering it unobservable: an unambiguous CGB detection would therefore pose a major threat to the inflatonay paradigm.

  • Adopt the working assumption that above the Planck scale point-like four-particle interactions involving two gravitons, whose rate at temperature T is of order \(\Gamma_g \sim T^5 / M_{\mathrm{Pl}^4} \), kept gravitons in thermal equilibrium in the primordial plasma. Detecting relics of a thermal gravitational wave background in the early Universe Primordial backgrounds of relic gravitons
  • Under these minimal assumptions the present-day CGB temperature is preficted to be \(T_{g,0} \simeq (3.91/106.75)^{1/3} T_{\gamma,0} \approx 0.9 K\), making the CGB 3 times colder than the CMB.

    by Jing Liu et al.

This work gives strict constraints on \(\alpha \) and \(\beta / H_*\) for the PT temperature below 100GeV, including dark PTs, QCD first-order PTs and electroweak first-order PTs.

    [ArXiv:2208.13999]Exploiting the Einstein Telescope to solve the Hubble tension

    by Matteo Califano et al.

    They demonstrate that the Einstein Telescope is capable of acheiving a relative accuracy below 1% on the Hubble constant independently of specific dark energy model. They first build mock catalogs containing gravitational wave events for one, five and ten years of observations, and above Signal-to-Noise Ratio equal to nine. They foresee that the Hubble constant is always constrained with less than 1% uncertainty, thereby offering a potential solution to the Hubble tension.

  • Mock data.
  • MCMC.
  • Go below \(\sim 0.1%\) with dark sirens, independently by the theoretical framework used in the statistical analysis.
