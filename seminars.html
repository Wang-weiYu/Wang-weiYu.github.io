    [ArXiv:2208.14901]The Gravitational-wave Optical Transient Observer (GOTO)

    by Martin J. Dyer et al.

    The Gravitational-wave Optical Transient Observer (GOTO) is a wide-field telescope project focused on detecting optical counterparts to gravitational-wave sources. As of 2022 the first two GOTO mounts have been commissioned at the Roque de los Muchachos Observatory on La Palma, Canary Islands, and construction of the second node with two additional 8-telescope mounts has begin at Siding Spring Observatory in New South Wales, Australia.

    [ArXiv:2208.14931]Impact of magnetic fields on Population III star formation

    by Cynthia R. Saad et al.

    The theory of the formation of the first stars in the Universe, the so-called Population III (Pop III), has until now largely negelected the impact of magnetic fields. They ascertain how the fragmentation properties of primordial protostellar discs are modified if magneto-hydrodynamic effects are present. In the present work, they have studied the effect of a magnetic field on the structure and evolution of the discs around Pop III stars inside a cosmological minihalo of \(M_{\mathrm{halo}} = 1.2 \times 10^6 M_\odot \), a virial radius of 130 pc, and a collapse (virializaiton) redshift of \(z \sim 25\).

  • For the runs without magnetic fields (hereafter termed hydro runs), the Piecewise Parabolic Method (PPM) is used.
  • To solve the cosmological MHD equations, the Harten-Lax-van Leer Riemann solver is employed.
  • The magnetic field is slowing the collapse. Considering the angular momentum profile, it is evident that the MHD case is particularly efficient in transporting angular momentum outwards.

    • [ArXiv:2208.14974]Full spectrum fitting with photometry in ppxf: non-parametric star formation history, metallicity and the quenching boundary from 3200 LEGA-C galaxies at redshift z~0.8

    by Michele Cappellari

    Updating to the PPXF method. Extract and compare the stellar population using three independent stellar population synthesis (SPS) methos and both photometric catalogues.

  • The PPXF software is available from https://pypi.org/project/ppxf/. The PPXF package contains a Python implementation of the Penelized PiXel-Fitting (pPXF) method to fit the stellar and gas kinematics, as well as the stellar population and the photometry (SED) of galaxies.
  • PPXF method, which has been used to measure stellar and gas kinematics as well as the formation history (SFH) and chemical composition of galaxies.
  • Backage other: HYPERZ, BPZ, LEPHARZ, ZEBEA and EAZY. These methods are conceptually similar to the template-based spectral fitting ones used for nearby galaxies, however, they all adopt a Bayesian approach, instead of a least-square fitting one. This makes the codes simpler and allows for easy inclusion of priors on galaxy parameters or non-Gaussian uncertainties.
  • Some fot the photometric-redshift methods were subsequently extended and new software were develepoed to fit spectra together with the photometry, while still retaining the same Bayesian approach. FAST, BESGLE, BAGPIPES the code described by Mendel et al. and PROSPECTOR.

    • [ArXiv:2208.14978]Primordial black holes and gravitational waves from dissipation during inflation

    by Guillermo Ballesteroset al.

    They study the generation of a localized peak in the primordial spectrum of curvature perturbations from a transient dissipative phase during inflation, leading to a large population of primordial black holes. The enhancement of the power spectrum occurs due to stochastic thermal noise sourcing curvature fluctuations.

    In their case, the effect of dissipation on the homegeneous inflaton dynamics can be described under adequate conditions by introducing a dissipative coefficient \(\Gamma\) in its time evolution. $$\ddot{\phi} + (3H+\Gamma)\dot{\phi} + V_\phi = 0$$ An enhanced \(\mathcal{P}_{\mathcal{R}}\) can arise in a strongly dissipative regime due to a stochastic source for the fluctuations coming from a thermal bath originating from the couplings between the inflaton and other fields that are also responsible for \(\Gamma\). The perturbation equations are driven by a source of noise with amplitude \(\sim \sqrt{\Gamma T}\), and so they are significantly enhanced whenever \(\Gamma\) is sufficiently large. The power spectrum of the comoving curvature perturbation is enhanced thanks to the source \(\delta \rho_r\) (whose value is set by the thermal noise) in the equation of motion for \(\delta \phi\).

    • [ArXiv:2208.14469]Exploiting stellar explosion induced by the QCD phase transition in large-scale neutrino detectors

    by Tetyana Pitiket al.

    The centers of the core-collapse supernovae are one of the densest environments in the Universe. Under such conditions, it is conceivable that a first-order phase transition from ordinary unclear matter to the quark-gluon plasma occurs. This transistion releases a large amount of latent heat that can drive a supernova explosion and may imprint a sharp signature in the neutrino signal. They show how this snap featur, if observed at large-scale neutrino detectors, can set competitive limits on neutrino masses and assist the localization of the supernova via triangulation.
