    [ArXiv:2209.00827]Constraining primordial tensor features with the anisotropies of the Cosmic Microwave Background

    by Jan Hamanna and Ameek Malhotra

    It is commonly assumed that the stochastic background of gravitational waves on cosmological scales follows an almost scale-independent power spectrum, as generically predicted by the inflationary paradigm. They do not find any evidence for the existence of bump-like tensor features at present, but demonstrate that future CMB experiments such as LiteBIRD and CMBS4 will greatly improve their prospects of determining the amplitude, location and width of such a bump.

  • spectator axion-gauge fields during inflation.
  • Using the temperature and polarisation maps from the Planck and BICEP/Keck datasets.
  • A log-normal bump in the primordial tensor spectrum at CMB scales.
  • MCMC.
  • The temperature and polarisation anisotropy.

    • [ArXiv:2209.00795]Propagation of scalar and tensor gravitational waves in Horndeski theory

    by Kei-ichiro Kubota et al.

    Gravitational waves travel through the distributions of matter and dark energy during propagation. They suggest a formultion for higher-order correlations of the geometrical optics expansion, applied to Horndeski theory which accommodates many dark energy models. They anallyze the equations of motion for scalar and tensor components of gravitational waves at the leading and next-to-leading oder in the geometrical optics expansion, deriving the evolution equations for their amplitudes under certain conditions. To study the phenomenology of the propagation of the metric and scalar perturbations over the curved background spacetime with distributions of matter and dark energy.

  • Horndeski theory is the most general theory of gravity in four dimensions whose Lagrangian is constructed out of the metric tensor and a scalar field and leads to second order equations of motion.
  • To probe the nature of dark energy.
  • The geometrical optics approximation.

    • [ArXiv:2209.01068]Emission of gravitational waves by superconducting cosmic strings

    by I. Yu. Rybak and L. Sousa

    They study the gravitational radiation emission efficiency \(\Gamma\) of superconducting cosmic strings. By using a solvable model of transonic strings, that the presence of a current leads to a suppression of the gravitational emission of cusps, kinks and different types of loops. They establish a phenomenological relationship between \(\Gamma\) and the value of current on the stochastic gravitational wave background generated by cosmic strings with additional degrees of freedom and show that both the amplitude and shape of the spectrum may be significantly affected.

    [ArXiv:2209.01107]Early dark energy by dark Higgs, and axion-induced non-thermal trapping

    by Shota Nakagawa et al.

    They propose a new scenario of early dark energy (EDE) with dark Higgs trapped at the origin. They us dark photons produced non-thermally by coherent oscillations of axions, which have a much stronger trapping effect than thermal mass. When the trapping ends, the dark Higgs quickly decays into dark photons, which are then red-shift as radiation. They find the viable parameter region of the axion decay constant and the axion mass where dark matter and the \(H_0\) tension can be simultaneously explained.

  • An ordinary Mexican-hat potential.
  • Can be QCD axion.
  • DM will be probed by various experiments such as ADMX, FLASH, DM-Radio and SRF.

    • [ArXiv:2209.00874]Gravitational waves and electromagnetic radiation from charged black hole binaries

    by Carlos A. Benavides-Gallego and Wen-Biao Han

    They analytically calculate fraviational and electromagnetic waveforms i the frequency domain for charged black hole binaries during the inspiral phase. In addition to the well-known \(f^{-7/6}\) waveforms, we also get a -11/6 power law gravitational wave component.

  • In the case of dipole radiation, the frequency-domain waves are proportional to \(f^{-7/6}\), while \(f^{-1/6}\) appears in the quadrupole contribution.
  • Deducion of waveforms.

    • [ArXiv:2209.01038]Multi-scalar Gauss-Bonnet gravity: scalarized black holes beyond spontaneous scalarization

    by Kalin V. Staykov and Daniela D. Doneva

    A new nonlinear mechanism for black hole scalarization, different from the standard spontaneous scalarization, was demonstrated to exist for scalar Gauss-Bonnet theories in which no tachyonic instabilities can occur. The Schwarzschild black hole is linearly stable but instead nonlinear instability can kick-in. They extend on this idea in the case of multi-scalar Gauss-Bonnet gravity with exponential coupling functions of third and fourth leading order in the scalar field. They demonstrate numerically the existence of scalarized black holes for both coupling funcitons and for all possible maximally symmetic scalar field target spaces. They demonstrated the existence of the rencently discovered mechanism for nonlinear scalarization in multi-scalar Gauss-Bonnet (MSGB) gravity, extending previous results in the single scalar field theory.
