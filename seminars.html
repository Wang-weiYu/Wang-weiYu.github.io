    by Martin Lopez-Corredoira

    They examined the trend and dispersion of values of the meaurements of \(H_0\) during 43 years (1976-2019).

    [ArXiv:2210.06810]Modified gravity approaches to the cosmological constant problem

    by The FADE Collaboration: Heliudson Bernardo et al.

    They review how modifications of Einstein's general relativity could alleviate the different problems associated with it that resul from the interplay of classical gravity and quantum field theory.

  • Massive gravity introduces mass to the graviton;
  • Horndeski theories lead to the breaking of translational invariance of the vaccum;
  • Models with extra dimensions change the symmetries of the vaccum.
  • The Dvali-Gabadadze-Porrati gravity: Gravity induced on brane (you can find it from living reviews in relativity )

    [ArXiv:2210.06500]Determining the spin of light primordial black holes with Hawking radiation

    by Marco Calzà and João G. Rosa

    They propose a method to determine the mass and spin of primordial black holes (PBHs) in the mass range \(5 \times 10^7 - 10^{12}\) kg (Hawking tempertures \(\sim\) 10 MeV), based on measuring the primary and secondary components.

    [ArXiv:2210.06861]Lifetime of scalar cloud formation around a rotating regular black hole

    by Mohsen Khodadi and Reza Pourkhodabakhshi

  • The scalar cloud, forms a gravitational atom with hydrogen-like bound states, lying on the threshold of a massive scalar field's supperadiant instability regime (time-growing quasi-bound states) and beyond (time-decaying quasi-bound states).
  • The hydrogen-like approximation, we shown a scalar cloud around the regularized Simpson-Visser black hole, forms faster than the standard Kerr case.
  • More detailed, adding the regularization length scale into the Kerr metric causes the timescale of the formation of a scalar cloud around a rotating black hole to become shorter (from one (O(10)) to a few orders of magnitude, depending on the values set for \(\epsilon\) ).
