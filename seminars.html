Seminars

I love seminars. In seminars, we talk, learn and laugh.

On Thursdays afternoon, 2:30 pm, I host a group seminar. In this seminar, we in our groups will share some interesting results from our works.

    [ArXiv:2210.10790]Strong Lensing by Galaxies

    by A. J. Shajib et al.

  • The most common methodologies to model lensing observables of galaxy-scale strong lens, especially the imaging data due to it being the most avaible and informative source of lensing observables.
  • They main results from literature in astrophysical and cosmological applications of galaxy-scale strong lens.
  • The current limitations of the data and methodologies.
  • A future outlook of the expected development and improvements in both aspects in the near future.

    • [ArXiv:2210.10793]LeMoN: Lens Modelling with Neural networks -- I. Automated modelling of strong gravitational lenses with Bayesian Neural Networks

    by Fabrizio Gentile et al.

  • LeMoN (Lens Modelling with Neural networks): a new machine-learning algorithm able to analyse hundreds of thousands of gravitational lenses in a reasonable amount of time.
  • The gravitational lenses expected from new-generation facilities.
  • They apply the LeMoN algorithm to pilot dataset of real lenses observed with HST during the SLACS program, obtaining unbiased estimates of the SIE parameters.
  • Code avaible in https://github.com/fab-gentile/LeMoN.

    • [ArXiv:2210.10893]Inference of gravitational lensing and patchy reionization with future CMB data

    by Federico Bianchini and Marius Millea

  • An optimal Bayesian solution for jointly secondary signals in the CMB originating from gravitational lensing and from patchy screening during the epoch of reionization.
  • A detection \( (3 \sigma) \) of the cross-correlation between lensing and screening is possible with SPT-3G.
  • A detection of the auto-correlation is possible with CMB-S4.

    • [ArXiv:2210.10931]Search for gravitational-wave transients associated with magnetar bursts in Advanced LIGO and Advanced Virgo data from the third observing run

    by LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA

  • The results of a search for short-duration (miliseconds to seconds) and long-duration (\(\sim 100\)s) transient gravitational waves from 13 magnetar short bursts observed during the O3.
  • No evidence of gravitational waves associated with the 16 bursts.
