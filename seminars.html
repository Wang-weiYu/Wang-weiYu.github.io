    [ArXiv:2210.05658]Lensing of gravitational waves: efficient wave-optics methods and validation with symmetric lenses

    by Giovanni Tambalo et al.

  • Wave optics (WO) effects are relevant at low frequencies, when the wavelength is comparable to the characteristic lensing time delay multiplied by the speed of light, and are thus often negligible for electromagnetic signals. Accurate predictions require computing the conditionally convergent diffraction integral, which involves highly oscillatory integrands and is numerically difficult.
  • They develop and implement several methods to compute lensing predictions in the WO regime valid for general gravitational lenses.
  • They derive approximations for high and low frequencies, obtaining explicit expressions for several analytic lens models.
  • They discuss two numerical methods suitable in the intermediate frequency range:
  • Regularized contour flow yields accurate answers in a fraction of a second for a broad range of frequencies.
  • Complex deformation is slower, but requires no knowledge of solutions to the geometric lens equation.
  • Use a regularization used for the saddle points.
  • Bining in time delays.

    • [ArXiv:2210.04916]The relation between globular cluster systems and supermassive black holes in spiral galaxies III. The link to the M∙−M∗ correlation

    by Rosa A. González-Lópezlira et al.

  • The relationship between globular cluster total number, and center black hole mass in spiral galaxies.
  • log M∙ \( \propto (1.01 \pm 0.13) \) log NGC for ellipticals,
  • log M∙ \( \propto (1.64 \pm 0.24) \) log NGC for ate type galaxies (LTG),
  • log M∙ \( \propto (1.48 \pm 0.18) \) log M∗ for ellipticals,
  • log M∙ \( \propto (1.21 \pm 0.16) \) log M∗ for ate type galaxies (LTG),
  • The observed agreement between ellipticals and LTG in this parameter space may imply that black holes and galaxies co-evolve through "calm" accretion, AGN feedback, and other secular processes.

    • [ArXiv:2210.05012]Three-dimensional core-collapse supernovae with complex magnetic structures: II. Rotational instabilities and multimessenger signatures

    by Matteo Bugli et al.

  • The gravitational collapse of rapidly rotating massive stars can lead to the onset of the low \(T/ ||W||\) instability within the central proto-neutron star (PNS), which leaves strong signatures in both the gravitational wave (GW) and neutrino emission.
  • We analyze a series of three-dimensional magnetohydrodynamic models to characterize the effects of different magnetic configurations on the development of the low \(T/ ||W||\) and the related multi-messenger features.
  • In the absence of magnetic fields - a strong burst of GW and a correlated modulation of the neutrino emission.
