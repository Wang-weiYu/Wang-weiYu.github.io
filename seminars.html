    • arXiv I interested everyday

    [ArXiv:2211.03814]Black Holes as "Time Capsules": A Cosmological Graviton Background and the Hubble Tension

    by Tsvi Piran and Raul Jimenez

  • Minuscule primordial black holes.
  • Gravitions produced during evaporation behave as "dark radiation".
  • With suitable parameters, this background graviton field can resolve the Hubble tension.

    • [ArXiv:2211.03947]Final stage of merging binaries of supermassive black holes: observational signatures

    by Jian-Min Wang et al.

  • Fates of mini-disks around component SMBHs.
  • A new instability, denoted as sound instability.

    • [ArXiv:2211.04058]Finding the missing baryons in the intergalactic medium with localized fast radio bursts

    by K. B. Yang et al.

  • The missing baryon problem.
  • Fast radio bursts.
  • They measure the baryon content of the Universe using 22 localized FRBs.
  • Considering the probability distributions of dispersion.
  • Their work supports that the baryons are not missing, but residing in the IGM.

    • [ArXiv:2211.04087]Big bang nucleosynthesis and early dark energy in light of the EMPRESS Yp results and the H0 tension

    by Tomo Takahashi and Sora Yamashita

    The exsitence of dark energy can reduce some tension implied by the EMPRESS Yp results.

    [ArXiv:2211.02661]White dwarfs as a probe of light QCD axions

    by Reuven Balkin et al.

    The modifications of the mass-radius relationship of white dwarfs allow us to probe large regions of unexplored axion parameter space without requiring it to be a significant fraction of dark matter.

    [ArXiv:2211.03799]Improved hot dark matter bound on the QCD axion

    by Alessio Notari et al.

  • A phenomenological production rate derived from pion-pion scattering data, overcoming the breakdown of chiral perturbation theory.

    • [ArXiv:2211.03845]SuperRad: A black hole superradiance gravitational waveform model

    by Nils Siemonsen et al.

  • Gravitational signatures of black hole superradiance are a unique probe of ultralight particles that are weakly-coupled to ordinary matter.
  • A new gravitational waveform model, SuperRad, that models the dynamics, oscillation frequency, and gravitational wave signals of thse clouds by combining numerical results in the relativistic regime with fits calibrated to analytical estimates, covering the entire parameter space of ultralight scalar and vector clouds with the lowest two azimuthal numers.

    • [ArXiv:2211.04106]On phase transitions near black holes

    by A. A. Grib and Yu. V. Pavlov

    It has been shown that temperatures near the horizon of rotating black holes can be about the phase transition temperature in the Standard Model with the Higgs boson. The distance from the horizon and gravitational and electromagnetic radiation emitted in collisions between particles have been numerically estimated.
