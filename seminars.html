    [ArXiv:2209.06831]New metrics to probe the dynamical state of galaxy clusters

    by Giulia Cerini et al.

    The present new diagnostic metrics to probe the dynamical state of galaxy clusters. These novel metrics rely on the computation of the power spectra of the matter and gas distributions and their corss-correlation derived from cluster observations. This analysis permits us to cross-correlate the fluctuations in the matter distribution, inferred from high-resolution lensing mass maps derived from HST data, with those derived from the emitted X-ray surface brightness distribution of the hot Intra-Cluster medium from the Chandra X-ray Observatory.

  • They present a new method for probing the dynamical state of galaxy clusters, based on the cross-correlation of the X-ray surface brightness and the mass distribution, the latter reconstructed from lensing observations.
  • The mass and X-ray auto-power spectra serve as new effective metrics.
  • The new quantity gas-mass coherence provides deeper insights into the dynamical state of galaxy clusters.
  • Mass maps reconstructed

    • [ArXiv:2209.06960]Comparison of star formation histories of AGN and non-AGN galaxies

    by G. Mountrichas et al.

    Their analysis shows that BD AGN tend to have younger stellar populations compared to BD, non-AGN systems. On the other hand, non-BD AGN have, on average, older stellar populations and are less likely to have experienced a burst compared to non-BD sources in the reference sample. Furthermore, AGN tend to prefer more compact systems compared to non-AGN.

    [ArXiv:2209.02741]Probing ultralight scalar, vector and tensor dark matter with pulsar timing arrays

    by Caner Unal et al.

  • Pulsar timing arrays (PTAs) are sensitive to oscillations in the gravitational potential along the line-of-sight due to ultralight particle pressure. They demonstrate that existing 15-year PTA data can robustly detect or rule out an ultralight component in the mass range \(10^{-26} - 10^{-23} \)eV with fraction down to precent level of total dark matter.
  • They also generalize the analysis and derive predictions for probing ultralight vector (i.e. dark photon) and spin-2 dark matter components.
  • Non-detection, together with other bounds in different mass regimes, will imply that ultralight scalar / axion can comprise at most 1 - 10% of dark matter in the \(10^{-30} - 10^{-17}\) eV regime.
  • With 30 years of observation, current PTAs can extend the reach down to 0.1%, while next-generation PTAs such SKA can probe the 0.01% level.
  • SKA OBSERVATORY
  • Ultralight particles are intriguing dark matter candidates, motivated by high energy theories. As they have de-Broglie wavelengths comparable to galactic or even larger cluster scales, denpending on their mass, they leave marks on cosmological and astrophysical observables.
  • How to detect: One such effect comes from the pressure of the ultralight field which fluctuates the energy-momentum tensor and equivalently spacetime. - modify the observed arrival time of pulses from pulsars and can be used to extract the properties.
  • $$\delta g_{00} = - 2 \Phi, \qquad \delta g_{ii} = 2 \psi \delta_{ij}$$
  • $$\psi = \psi_0 (x) + \pi G_N \rho / m^2 \cos(2mt + 2\alpha)$$
  • The time residual can be calculated via fractional frequency shift as $$\delta \Delta t = \int_{t_p}^{t} \frac{v' - \bar{v}}{\bar{v}}dt$$ where \(t\) and \(t_p\) are the time at Earth and pulsar emission.
  • The correlation coefficient between each pair of pulsars separated by an angle \(\zeta\)
  • Scalar: \(\chi_{IJ} = 1\)
  • Vector: \(\chi_{IJ} = \frac{1}{3} \)
  • The sensitivity curve of PTA is different depending on the frequency range.
  • \(f > 1/T_{obs}\) white timing noise, \(S_n (f) = 12 \pi^2 f^2 2 \Delta t \sigma^2\)
  • \(f < 1/T_{obs}\) red noise
