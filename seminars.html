    • arXiv I interested everyday

    [ArXiv:2210.07248]Exploring the cosmological synergy between galaxy cluster and cosmic void number counts

    by Davide Pelliciari et al.

  • The constraints on \(\Omega_m\) and \(\sigma_8\).
  • They found the cluster and void counts represent powerful independent and complementary probes to test the cosmological framework.
  • Increasing the \(S_8\) constraint precision by a factor of about 60 %.
  • Simulation.

    • [ArXiv:2210.07304]Beyond ΛCDM constraints from the full shape clustering measurements from BOSS and eBOSS

    by Agne Semenaite et al.

  • They obtain constraints on the cosmological parameters independent of the Hubble parameter \(h\) for the extensions of the \(\Lambda\)CDM models, focusing on cosmologies with free dark energy equation of state parameter \(w\).
  • Many parameters except for \(sigama_{12}\) (\(2.4 \sigma\)) are consistent with the LCDM.
  • Their physical curvature density constraint for Planck \(\omega_k = -0.0116^{+0.0029}_{-0.0036}\) prefers a curved Universe at \(4 \sigma\) significance, which is \(2.4 \sigma\) higher than what is found using \(\Omega_K\).

    • [ArXiv:2210.07779]Robust Supermassive Black Hole Spin Mass-Energy Characteristics: A New Method and Results

    by Ruth A. Daly

  • The traditional method: the relationship between the dimensionless spin angular momentum \(j\) and the normalized spin energy is highly non-linear, the empirically determined values of \(j\) cannot exceed unity dut to the term \(\sqrt{1-j^2}\) even though observational uncertainties require this flexibility.
  • The new method: Relationships between the black hole spin mass-energy characteristics and the black hole spin function \(F^2\) are obtained. A linear relationship between the black hole spin function and the normalized spin mass-energy of the black hole, which approximate \(F^2\). In the method, the exponent of \(F\) can vary according to \(E_\mathrm{spin}/E_\mathrm{spin,max}\approx 1.75 \log(F)\).

    • [ArXiv:2210.08002]Tidal Disruption of Main-Sequence Stars by Intermediate-Mass Black Holes

    by Fulya Kıroğlu

  • One sum mass middel-age main-squence star (MESA) with massive black holes (\(M_\mathrm{BH} = 100 - 19^4 M_\odot\)) through hydrodynamic simulation.
  • After an initial encounter right at the classical tidal disruption limit, \(1 M_\odot\) star undergoes 16(5) pericenter passages before ejection from a \(10 M_\odot\) (\(100 M_\odot\)) black hole.
  • Observations of consecutive electromagnetic flares from these repeated close passages could in principle be used to determine the mass of the black hole, thus possibly proving the existence of intermediate-mass black holes.
