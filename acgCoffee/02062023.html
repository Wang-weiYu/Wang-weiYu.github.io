[ArXiv:2306.12468]Measuring the Hubble constant with kilonovae using the Expanding Photosphere Method

by Albert Sneppen et al.

Kilonovae associated with mergers can provide an independent and potentially percent-accurate distance measurement to individual mergers.

  • They quantify the cosmological constraining power of the "lensing PDF" - the one-point probability density of weak lensing convergence maps - by modelling this statistic numerically with an emulator trained on \(\omega\)CDM cosmic shear simulations.
  • In the Stage-IV scenario the lensing PDFs contain all information from the standard two-point statistic and more. This suggests that while these two probes are currently complementray, the lower noise levels of upcoming surveys will unleash the constraining power of the PDF.
