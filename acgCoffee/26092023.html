[ArXiv:2309.13100]JWST early Universe observations and \(\Lambda CDM\) Cosmology

by Rajendra Gupta

One could infer the CCC model as an extension of the \(\Lambda CDM\) model with a dynamic cosmological cosntant.

In CCC model, the luminosity distance could be written as \(d_p(t_0) = c\int_0^z \frac{dz}{H(z)} = c \int_0^z \frac{dz}{(H_0 + \alpha) (1+z)^{3/2} f(z)^{-(1/2)}} - \alpha\).

Most trouble in high redshift, for there is no much room for Cosmology in the late-time Universe.

[ArXiv:2309.13295]Sesame: A power spectrum emulator pipeline for beyond-\(\Lambda CDM\) models

by Renate Mauland et al.

This tool, named Sesame, can be usde by anyone to generate a power spectrum emulator for cosmological model of their choice.

  • Fast approximate N-body simulations.
  • Run a Boltzmann solver to produce initial conditions
  • Machine Learning
https://github/com/renmau/Sesame\_pipline

[ArXiv:2309.13359]Analysis of the Gravitational Wave Background Using Gamma-Ray Pulsar Timing Arrays with Next-Generation Detectors

by Zhen Xie et al.

They investigate the potential of gamma-ray pulsar time array (PTA) on gravitaional waves background (GWB) using future gamma-ray detectors with larger effective areas. The gamma PTA shows great potential to match radio PTA in a decade. The gamma PTA is a supplementation and cross-checking tool for radio PTA.

[ArXiv:2309.13608]Determining cosmological-model-independent \(H_0\) and post-Newtonian parameter with time-delay lenses and supernovae

by Tonghua Liu and Kai Liao

They propose a model-independent method for simultaneous constraining on Hubble constant and post-Newtonian parameter ( \(\gamma_{ppn}\) ) using strong lensing systems and observational SNe Ia. Our results are \(H_0 = 72.9^{+2.0}_{-2.3} km/s/Mpc\) and \(\gamma_{ppn} = 0.89^{+0.17}_{-0.15}\). All the lenses show that there is no obvious evidence to support GR deviation with observational uncertainties.

\(D_{\Delta t}/D'_d\) to void the influence of \(H_0\) on GR testing. The Hubble constant and PPN parameter are in fact completely degenerate together. You can see [arXiv:2003.03277].

[ArXiv:2309.13946]Observational constraints on interactions between dark energy and dark matter with momentum and energy transfers

by Xiaolin Liu et al.

The two couplings are constrained to be \(\beta = 0.417^{+1.592}_{-0.307}\) and \(Q = -0.036^{+0.036}_{-0.010}\).
