[ArXiv:2309.11537]GWAK: Gravitational-Wave Anomalous Knowledge with Recurrent Autoencoders

by Ryan Raikman et al.

What is the Recurrent Autoencoders? First of all, what is the autoencoder. In short, it is a type of artificial neural network which combine elements of recurrent neural network (RNNS) and autoencoders

With supervised DL approaches, there is no guarantee of generalizability to an out-of-training event, what we refer to as ``anomalies''.

The package website: https://github.com/ML4GW/gwak.

They created five different classes of data as proxies for signal and background signatures:
  • Binaries - Simulated BBH signals using IMRPhenomPv2 injected into the real background noise
  • Background - Background from O3 with DQsecDB state flag DCS-ANALYSIS\_READY\_CO:1 applied and excess power gliches and known GW-events removed.
  • Sin-Gaussain 64 - 512 Hz - Generic how frequency signal model used to simulate generic GW sources.
  • Sin-Gaussain 512 - 1024 Hz - Generic high frequency signal model used to simulat generic GW sources.
  • Glitches - Transient instrumental gliches (often of unknown origin) flagged by omicron as having excess power.

They found that the GWAK method efficiently detected anomalies in GW datasets. In particular, unmodelled sources like core-collapse supernovae and white noise bursts. What's more, the GWAK method could differentiate signal-like anomalies from anomalous events, such as those resulting from detector glitches.

[ArXiv:2309.11552] The many tensions with dark-matter based models and implications on the nature of the Universe

by Pavel Kroupa et al.

This updated review has two purposes: to explore new tensions that have arisen in recent years, compounding the unresolved tensions from previous studies, and to use the shortcomings of the current theory to guide the development of a successful mode.

In the light of the growing evidence for large-(>few hundred Mpc)-scale inhomogeneities, they have shown that the Hubble Tension is simply caused by the matter bulk flows of the large scale structure. These observations suggest that the Universe is more inhomogeneous and that structures grow more rapidly than what is allowed by the SMoC on the Gpc scale.
