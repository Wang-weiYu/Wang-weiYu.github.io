arXiv I interested everyday

[ArXiv:2311.12926]An Anti-halo void Catalogue of the Local Super-Volume

by Stephen Stopyra et al.

They construct an anti-halo void catalogue of 150 voids with radii R greater than 10 h^-1 Mpc in the Local Super-Volume ( less than 135 h^-1 Mpc from the Milky Way)

[ArXiv:2311.12928]Void Probability Function inside cosmic voids: evidence for hierarchical scaling of high-order correlations in real space

by Federico Davila-Kurban et al.

They compare the reduced void probability function inside and outside of cosmic voids in the TNG300-1 simulation, both in real and simulated redshift space. The negative binomial model is not valid in real space.

[ArXiv:2311.13305]A tale of many H0

by Licia Verde et al.

A review.

[ArXiv:2311.13498]Unveiling Omegam0 is independently: a journey and consistency quest with first-order perturbation theory

by Bikash R.Dinda

They employ Gaussian process regression to reconstrct Hubble parameter and growth rate values. Their cosmological model-independent approach offers valuable constraints on Omegam0, affirming the constistency of FLRW and Newtonian perturbation theory.

[ArXiv:2311.12925]Are f(R, Matter) theories really relevant to cosmology?

by Osmin Lacombe et al.

f(R, Matter) theories couple the curvature R or Rmunu with the matter sector in the action. If these theories genuinely couple the SM to curvature, they suffer from the presence of ghost states at energies within their domain of application for cosmology purposes.

[ArXiv:2311.12985]Gravitational Waves from Thurston Geometries

by Daniel FLores-Alfonso et al.

Find the Gravitational Waves in these theories.

[ArXiv:2311.13264]Gravitational repulsive effects in 3D regular black holes

by Orlando Luongo et al.

Considering the effects of repulsive gravity in an invariant way for four static 3D regular black holes, using the eigenvalues of the Riemann curvature tensor, the Ricci scalar, and the strong energy conditions.

[ArXiv:2311.13300]Effect of Noise Estimation in Time-Domain Ringdown Analysis: A case Study with GW150914

by Hai-TIan Wang and Lijing Shao

No strong evidence for overtones in GW150914.

[ArXiv:2311.13563]Detection of the relativistic Shapiro delay in a highly inclined

by T. Gautam et al.
