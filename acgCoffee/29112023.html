[ArXiv:2311.16225]A Systematic Review of 24 Previously Measured Black Hole Spins in X-ray Binaries

by Paul A. Draghis et al.

In some cases, different spectra from the same source yield similar spin measurements but conflicting values for the inclination of the inner disk; they suggest that this is due to variable disk winds obsecuring the bule wing of relativistic Fe K emission line.

[ArXiv:2311.16830]The Role of Type Ia Supernovae in Constraining the Hubble Constant

by Daniel Scolnic and Maria Vincenzi

They conclude given all available evidence, it is difficult to find a way that a systematic in supernovae measurements, or a non-LCDM component of the universe which could be measured with supernovae, can help explain the Hubble tension.

[ArXiv:2311.16862]Solving the Hubble tension at intermediate redshifts with dynamical dark energy

by Isaac Tutusaus et al.

They 'solve' this problem by introducing a redshift-dependent systematic effect that brings the luminosity distance into agreement with the angular diameter distance. The resulting expansion history is no longer compatible with LCDM, but this can be fixed with a dynamical dark energy component. In this way, they are able to solve the Hubble tension at intermediate redshifts.
